# Devil's Advocate: the Digital Ecosystem Action Plan

> Adversarial review of `digital-ecosystem-action-plan.md`, run with Hugo on 2026-06-18. Seven challenges, every one held up and produced a change. The plan and the Lucy-facing PDF were revised to match.
> Voice rule: no em dashes, no en dashes.

---

## The big new fact that surfaced

Lucy's personal Instagram (@lucywayne__) has about **900 followers**. So there is no existing audience to "funnel." The whole plan was quietly assuming her personal brand was a distribution channel. It is not yet. It is the trust and the face, not the reach. This reframes several challenges below.

---

## Challenge 1: Lucy is the single point of failure

**Risk:** we made Lucy both the reason to buy and the entire content engine, with no product fallback. If she goes quiet, the brand goes quiet.

**Decided:** Lucy's realistic capacity is **moderate, needs structure**. So we do not run on her posting live every few days. We **batch-film** on shoot days, Hugo writes her an **exact shot list** each time, we keep a **2 to 3 week content buffer**, and **ambassador, product, and customer content carry about half the feed** so she is never the only fuel. Cadence is one she can hold, not a sprint.

## Challenge 2: Is the personal audience real?

**Risk:** the "leverage her personal brand" thesis assumed a following worth funneling.

**Decided:** at ~900 followers, **there is nothing to funnel yet**. Reframe the premise from "borrow Lucy's audience" to "**build** one from close to scratch, using her credibility (PT, stylist, press) as the trust and the content." Do not build the separate Lucy Wayne website funnel.

## Challenge 3: Too many properties for a two-person team

**Risk:** a Lucy site, a Sportif site, two Instagram accounts, TikTok, Facebook, email, and wholesale is too much surface area, and splitting a near-zero audience across two accounts grows it slower.

**Decided:** **collapse to one hub.** Everything behind **@sportifcollection with Lucy as the face**, plus TikTok, Shopify, and email. **No separate Lucy website.** @lucywayne__ stays a light personal account that cross-posts now and then. (This walks back the "Lucy Wayne landing page" idea from the meeting, flag to Lucy.)

## Challenge 4: "Paid later" may starve the build phase

**Risk:** 900 followers to a 10,000 goal by September, organic-only, when organic reach is basically dead, does not add up.

**Decided:** **seeding becomes the main growth engine** (ambassadors and instructors lend us the audience we do not have), AND we **bring a small paid layer forward into the build phase**, aimed at follows and email signups rather than sales. **Re-baseline the 10k goal** to an honest target (a smaller, genuinely engaged audience plus a solid email list). The bigger sales-focused paid still waits for launch.

## Challenge 5: 500 units versus the demand machine, and a 35-day reorder

**Risk:** sell out in week one, then go dark for five weeks waiting on China, right at peak momentum.

**Decided:** **founding drop + waitlist + early reorder.** Frame the 500 as a deliberate limited founding drop, switch the product page to a **back-in-stock or pre-order waitlist** when stock runs low so a sellout builds the list, and have Lucy **commit to batch 2 early** so the gap is short. The early reorder depends on her cash, so it is on the approval list.

## Challenge 6: Over-betting on TikTok

**Risk:** real effort aimed at TikTok and an uncontrolled TikTok Shop Australia date, for a customer who skews Instagram.

**Decided (Hugo deferred, Claude's call):** **Instagram plus email is the spine**, TikTok stays **light on repurposed content**, and TikTok Shop is treated as **upside if and when it lands**, not a pillar. Keeps Lucy's limited content time where her customer actually buys.

## Challenge 7: Wholesale is "parallel" on paper but barely in the plan

**Risk:** months spent building a cold audience while Lucy's warm gym relationships (faster-converting) sit underused.

**Decided (Hugo deferred, Claude's call):** **elevate wholesale to a real workstream** and treat it as the faster near-term revenue alongside the DTC build. Build the line sheet, a simple lookbook, a named list of the gyms Lucy knows, wholesale pricing and a minimum order, and a short warm outreach sequence she runs. It de-risks everything else: if DTC is slow to ignite, wholesale is paying the bills and putting product in front of exactly the right people.

---

## Net effect on the plan

The plan got **simpler and more honest**: fewer properties, a realistic follower target, growth driven by other people's audiences plus a little early paid, a clear answer to the inventory gap, Instagram-first focus, and wholesale promoted from afterthought to front-loaded revenue. The single biggest shift is recognising that we are building an audience from ~900, not leveraging an existing one, so seeding and warm wholesale matter more than we first wrote.
