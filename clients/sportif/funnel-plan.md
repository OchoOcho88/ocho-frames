# Sportif Funnel Plan

> How the funnel playbook applies to Sportif's September 2026 launch. General principles live in `docs/funnel-playbook.md`; this doc is only what Sportif actually builds.
> Internal working doc (Hugo + Claude). Not Lucy-facing yet; a client cut can come later once decisions here are made.
> Read `brand.md` guardrails before writing any funnel copy. **The Donati health-claim examples are exactly what Sportif must NOT say.**
> **Created:** 2026-07-11 (Session 017, Cowork)

## 1. The one big translation

Donati's framework assumes an info or service product with health-outcome headlines ("How to get a 6 pack without giving up your favourite foods"). Sportif cannot make outcome claims (brand guardrail + Meta Restricted Health and Wellness). So every formula gets translated from body outcomes to aesthetic, lifestyle, and feeling outcomes:

**How to [look/feel outcome] without [her objection].**

Working headline candidates (draft, test these):

- "How to actually look forward to your 6am pilates, without buying a whole new wardrobe."
- "Training accessories too fashionable to hide in the gym bag. Finally not black and fluro."
- "How to build a morning ritual you love, without the boring gear."

Her documented objections to slot into the "without" half: guilt about spending on herself ("buy fewer, beautiful things that last"), boredom with black and fluro, self-consciousness about the "long and lean" pilates aesthetic, busyness (easy wins).

## 2. What Sportif's funnels are (three, in order)

**Funnel 1: Waitlist funnel (NOW, pre-launch).** This is the Shopify coming-soon page already on the critical path, upgraded to a real funnel:

- One page, one action: join the waitlist (name + email, phone optional for SMS).
- Result-based headline (from section 1) + Lucy piece to camera, 60 to 90 seconds: where she was (PT and stylist who could not find beautiful accessories), what she made, when it lands. This is Donati's story arc, and it is also just Lucy being Lucy.
- Incentive: waitlist gets first access to the 500-unit first run + the gift-with-purchase pouch. Honest scarcity: 500 units, 35-day restock lead time. We never have to fake urgency, the supply chain provides it.
- Social proof placeholder: instructor seeding quotes and photos as they come in.
- Mobile first (her whole discovery journey is IG Reels and TikTok on a phone).

**Funnel 2: Launch funnel (September).** The band product page rebuilt as a funnel page, not a catalogue page:

- One hero product (the band), one call to action. The ankle strap is the "fries with that" at cart, not a competing hero.
- The pouch gift over the ~$70 threshold IS the Donati "compelling incentive," already designed. Frame it exactly as affinity buying: the order arrives as a gift to herself.
- Testimonials in outcome + objection format from the seeding cohort ("I thought it was just pretty, it is the only band I use now").
- Retargeting with a NEXT action: saw the Reel → show the unboxing; visited the page → show the pouch threshold; abandoned cart → email at 1 hour, SMS at 24 hours.

**Funnel 3: Educational trust funnel (ongoing, organic + light paid).** One ad or Reel per real FAQ (which colour, band vs strap, what do I actually use it for, is it worth it). Lucy answering one question per video is educational marketing, long-term trust, and content calendar all at once. This also feeds the "personal brand campaign, goal: build trust, educate" line from the notes: Lucy's page funnels to Sportif.

## 3. Email + SMS spine (wire BEFORE ads spend)

Per the playbook benchmarks, flows before campaigns:

1. **Welcome flow** (waitlist joins): 3 emails. Lucy's story, the ritual world, the launch promise. Expect the highest open rates of anything we ever send.
2. **Launch sequence** (waitlist, September): early access, then general.
3. **Abandoned cart**: email at 1 hour, SMS at 24 hours if unopened. Calm tone, no White Fox urgency spam; the honest 500-unit line does the work.
4. **Post-purchase**: unboxing-photo ask (the pouch earns the tag), review ask in outcome + objection format, restock notice.

Klaviyo is already the planned tool (see budget bands in `brand.md`). SMS is a later add; collect the phone field as optional from day one so the list is SMS-ready.

## 4. The Lucy session idea (from the summit notes)

Hugo's raw idea: "each band sale gets you a half hour session with Lucy."

Flag: at 500 bands that is 250 hours of Lucy's time, and the operations note in `brand.md` says the brand must not depend on Lucy's personal responsiveness. Keep the spirit, fix the scaling:

- **Adaptation A (recommended):** a monthly live group session (IG Live or in-person Bondi/Double Bay class) for customers. One hour of Lucy serves everyone, builds community (the 440 model), and creates recurring content.
- **Adaptation B:** the half-hour 1:1 as a top-tier incentive only (first 10 orders, or orders over a high threshold), where the scarcity is honest and the load is capped.
- **Adaptation C:** a styled "morning ritual" video series free to customers (evergreen, zero marginal cost).

Decision needed from Hugo/Lucy before launch copy references any of these.

## 5. Budget alignment

The playbook's $30 to 50 per day minimum test spend sits inside the existing Lean band (under 10k/month, `brand.md`). Sequence: organic + seeding first (already the plan), then small Meta tests on the waitlist funnel (lead objective, but send to the landing page rather than instant forms: at Sportif's price point we want intent, and the page IS the brand experience), 7 to 10 day tests, judge on cost per waitlist lead, then scale what works into September.

## 6. What we do NOT take from the summit

- No health or performance outcome claims in any headline or ad (guardrail).
- No hype urgency mechanics (countdown spam, constant discounts). Scarcity is stated calmly and only when true.
- No disqualifying quiz funnel at launch; that is a services pattern, overkill for a $30 to 70 AOV product. Revisit if a bundle-builder ever makes sense.
- No ChatGPT-surface ads; too early, budget too small.
