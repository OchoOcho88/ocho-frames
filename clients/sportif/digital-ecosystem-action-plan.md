# Sportif Digital Ecosystem and Action Plan

> How every digital piece links together (one Sportif hub: the Shopify store, @sportifcollection Instagram with Lucy as the face, TikTok, Facebook/Meta, email, and wholesale), and the step by step to build it. This is the working checklist. The Lucy-facing approval version is `Sportif-Digital-Plan-for-Lucy.pdf`.
> Built on the locked strategy: Lucy is the differentiator, parallel wholesale plus DTC.
> **Revised 2026-06-18 after a devil's advocate review (`digital-plan-devils-advocate.md`).** The big finding: Lucy's personal Instagram is only about 900 followers, so we are building an audience, not funneling an existing one. Net changes: collapse to one hub (no separate Lucy website), make ambassador and instructor seeding the main growth engine, bring a small paid layer forward for follows and email, set an honest follower target, handle inventory as a founding drop with a waitlist and an early reorder, keep Instagram and email as the spine with TikTok light, and elevate wholesale to a real front-loaded workstream.
> Research basis: our own market research (`research/au-segment-profile.md`, `research/brand-references.md`, `research/budget-benchmarks.md`, `research/cultural-lane-validation.md`) plus fresh 2026 platform best practices (see Sources at the end).
> Voice rule: no em dashes, no en dashes.

## The one principle

Put the things we OWN at the centre (the Shopify store and the email list) and make everything else feed them. Rented attention (followers, the algorithm, a gym shelf) is the top of the funnel. Owned assets (store, email) are where the value is kept. Lucy is the face and the trust (a founder brand converts far better than a faceless one), but with about 900 followers today she is not yet a distribution channel. So the honest job is to BUILD an audience from close to scratch, using two levers we can actually pull now: other people's audiences (ambassador and instructor seeding) and a little early paid reach. Warm wholesale (the gyms Lucy already knows) runs alongside as the faster near-term revenue.

## The ecosystem (how it all links)

**The home (owned core):**

- **Shopify store (the Sportif site).** The till and the home. Every link, every bio, every email points here. Even before launch it should exist as a "coming soon" page that captures emails.
- **Email list (Klaviyo on Shopify).** The owned audience. Seeded with Lucy's existing contact list, grown by the waitlist, kept warm by the welcome flow and a light monthly note.

**The hub (one brand presence, Lucy as the face):**

- **@sportifcollection (Instagram), the spine.** The main account, where we build ONE brand audience. Lucy is the recurring face. Product tags in Reels, the shop tab, the link in bio to the store and the list.
- **Lucy's existing personal presence is a launch asset, used as a light bridge (not rebuilt).** She already has `@lucywayne___` (826 followers, verified, "Personal Stylist & Certified Trainer"), the site lucywayne.com.au, and a Stan Store. The devil's advocate point stands: we do not pour effort into a second site or split the build across two places. Instead we use what exists to introduce Sportif to her warm, trusting audience: she announces Sportif to her followers, adds a Sportif link/mention to her bio and her personal site, and cross-posts now and then. The build effort stays on the one brand hub.

**The growth engines (how we get reach we do not have yet):**

- **Ambassadors and instructors, the MAIN growth lever.** Sydney pilates instructors and micro-creators seeded with bands, posting to THEIR audiences. With only ~900 of our own followers, their reach is the engine, not a nice-to-have.
- **A small early paid layer.** Brought forward into the build phase, aimed at follows and email signups (not sales). It buys the reach organic will not give us from a standing start.
- **TikTok, light.** Instagram is the spine for this customer; TikTok runs on repurposed clips (low effort). TikTok Shop Australia is upside if it lands, not a pillar.
- **Facebook / Meta, the ads and tracking engine.** Not for daily posting (a Page reaches only 1 to 6 percent of followers). Its job is the Meta pixel and the product catalogue, shared with Instagram through Shopify. A community Group is an option later.

**The faster near-term revenue (front-loaded, not parallel-and-forgotten):**

- **Wholesale, the gyms Lucy already knows.** Warm relationships convert faster than a cold audience, so this is a real workstream, not an afterthought: a line sheet, a simple lookbook, a named target list, wholesale pricing and a minimum order, and a short outreach sequence Lucy runs. Likely the first real revenue while the audience builds.

**The flow, in one line:** discover (Lucy plus ambassador content, a little paid) goes to click (link in bio) goes to land (Shopify plus email capture) goes to nurture (email) goes to buy goes to delight (the gift-with-purchase pouch, reviews, photos) goes to advocate (ambassadors and community) goes back to discover. Wholesale runs in parallel and feeds credibility back into all of it.

## Content for Lucy (what to post, and when)

Grounded in our own segment profile (`research/au-segment-profile.md`) and brand references (`research/brand-references.md`). This also answers the "need content ideas for Lucy" from the meeting.

**The archetypes that work for this brand** (borrowed from our two references, Anine Bing and Kikiva):

- **Founder diaries:** "come with me to a shoot," packing orders, dropping parcels at the post office, stock arrivals (the 500 bands landing is a perfect one to film).
- **The origin story:** "how I started this brand" and the why (sick of masculine black-and-fluro gear, the stylist eye).
- **Outfit and styling:** one set styled three ways, for the walk, the class, and the cafe after. Flat-lay and on-body.
- **Lookbook Reels:** short, calm, real movement.
- **Community reshares:** customer and ambassador photos, try-ons, tagged content.
- **Behind the scenes:** sampling, testing, choosing the colourway, packing the gift pouch.

Captions stay short and confident ("Everyday training, elevated," "For the long run," "Too fashionable not to WEAR"), never hypey. Muted, slightly desaturated grading, warm neutrals with the product as the pop of colour.

**When to post (built around her actual day):**

- **Early morning (5:30 to 8:30):** inspiration and motivation. She checks her phone on waking, before a walk or class.
- **Late afternoon:** shopping and decision content. She browses and decides later in the day.
- **Evening:** TikTok peaks here ("day in the life," routines, hauls).

The pattern that matters: she discovers on Reels and TikTok, saves and screenshots, and often buys later after an email. So the job is to stop her in the morning, capture the email, and convert by email later.

**Podcasts (the awareness play from the meeting):** her segment listens on walks and commutes, mostly wellness, self-development, business and entrepreneurship, and true crime. Target wellness and small-business or founder shows for Lucy.

## Budget and costs (from our own research)

Our AU benchmarks (`research/budget-benchmarks.md`), all in AUD.

- **The monthly tool stack (unavoidable running cost):** roughly **250 to 510 a month at launch** (Shopify 80 to 130, Klaviyo 60 to 120, a reviews app 30 to 60, a simple attribution tool 80 to 200), rising to about 310 to 660 as the list grows. This is the baseline cost of running the owned core, separate from ads.
- **Paid ads, when we turn them on (lean band):** under **10k a month total**, split roughly Instagram Reels 3 to 4k, TikTok 2.5 to 3.5k, retargeting 0.5 to 1k. At launch we start at the very bottom (retargeting first). The goal is to learn, not to scale. Mid band is 15 to 30k, scale is 50k+, neither of which fits a 500-unit first run.
- **Creators (the ambassador cohort):** a lean, micro-heavy cohort of 4 creators runs roughly **1.6 to 2.9k** including light paid-usage rights; a 6-creator mix is about 5 to 9k. Micro creators (10 to 50k followers) are the best value for bands. One launch-grade UGC video is about 500 to 800.
- **Reality check on returns:** a new brand usually loses a little on first-touch ads in month 1 (blended ROAS about 1.0 to 1.8x), improving to about 1.8 to 2.8x by month 3 as email kicks in (email becomes 15 to 25 percent of revenue). September is good timing: it is the baseline-cost month, cheaper than the November Black Friday surge and the January resolution rush, and it leads into the Australian summer. Sensible spend ceiling before the US move is about 30k a month.

## How we will measure it (keep it simple)

- **Email list size, plus open and click rates.** The leading indicator of launch-day sales.
- **Instagram and TikTok:** saves, shares, follows, and collab-post reach (not just likes).
- **Shopify:** sessions, add-to-cart, and conversion rate. Check the Meta pixel is firing the key events (PageView, ViewContent, AddToCart, InitiateCheckout, Purchase).
- **Sell-through against the 500 units**, and **branded searches** ("Sportif bands") as the sign the brand is pulling demand on its own.
- **UTM links** on every bio link so we know which platform actually drives sales.

## Operational note: who answers customers (added 2026-06-18)

A reconnaissance check found a real risk. An inquiry sent through Lucy's personal website went **unanswered for 7 days**, and her link-in-bio is fragmented (a Stan Store, two separate Calendly links, a TikTok). The lesson: Sportif cannot depend on Lucy personally answering messages quickly, and we must not route Sportif customers through her leaky personal channels.

So we design comms to NOT depend on her manual follow-up:

- **One monitored Sportif inbox** (the new Workspace email), with **Lauren [EA] owning day-to-day replies**.
- **Automated confirmations and flows in Klaviyo** (order confirmation, shipping updates, the welcome series), so the routine stuff answers itself instantly.
- Her **personal accounts act only as a one-link bridge** to Sportif. We do not collect or handle Sportif inquiries there.
- **Tidy her link-in-bio later** (add one clean Sportif link), but carefully, it is her personal business.

This is also a question to raise gently with Lucy: who is responsible for replying to customers, and how fast, because a slow or missed reply at launch costs sales.

## Why these choices (2026 research, for our record)

- **Personal brand first:** strong personal brands convert 3 to 7 times better; combining brand-building with conversion delivers 15 to 20 percent higher ROI than chasing conversions alone. (This is why Lucy is the face. But at ~900 followers she is the trust, not yet the reach, so seeding and a little early paid are what actually build the audience.)
- **Instagram:** the "link in bio" era is closing; product tags now live inside Reels (up to 30 per post), and tagged content sells far better. Collab posts merge audiences. Micro-creators get 3 to 5 times the engagement.
- **TikTok:** the customer skews Instagram and Lucy's content time is limited, so TikTok is a light, repurposed channel for now. TikTok Shop launches in Australia in 2026; treat it as upside if it lands, not a pillar.
- **Facebook:** Page organic reach is 1 to 6 percent, so use Meta as the ads and tracking engine (pixel plus catalogue) and consider a Group, not a Page feed, for community.
- **Email:** the welcome flow is the highest-converting window (orders at roughly 2.3 percent of sends, about 18 times a normal email), and the first email must land within minutes.
- **Link in bio:** keep it a simple router to the Shopify store and the email list (Linktree free tier or Shopify's own; Stan Store is an option if Lucy later wants a creator storefront).
