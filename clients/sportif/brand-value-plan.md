# Sportif Brand Value Plan

> How we build long-term brand value for Sportif. Plain language, founder-first.
> Source of truth. Read alongside `brand.md` and `campaigns/launch-2026-09/synthesis-brief.md`.
> Voice rule: no em dashes, no en dashes.
> Created: 2026-06-17. Updated 2026-06-18 after Hugo's in-person meeting with Lucy (Session 011): go-to-market is parallel wholesale plus DTC, the community mechanic is ambassador and instructor seeding (440 Run Club model), and the band is the hero presented inside a giftable set.

---

## The idea in one line

Brand value is the reason someone picks Sportif and pays a little more, instead of buying an almost identical band somewhere cheaper. The product can be copied. The brand cannot. This is now even truer than when we wrote it: as of 2026-06-18 we know the product is a standard factory item (predefined colourways, no custom pattern), so the band itself is NOT the edge. The lasting edge is the brand around it, and above all Lucy Wayne herself. This plan is how we build that, six levers, each matched to Sportif, with Lever 2 (Lucy as the face) now the most important of the six.

---

## Lever 1: A look people recognise instantly

**The idea:** value starts with a look so consistent that someone knows it is yours before they see the logo.

**For Sportif:** the warm-neutral world (linen, cream, sand) with the product as the one pop of colour, in our palette (Blush Peach `#F0CDB3`, caramel, terracotta, chocolate), the Glacial Indifference wordmark, and, most of all, Lucy herself as a recurring face in the frame. **Note (2026-06-18):** we had hoped a pattern or woven texture would be the recognisable signature, but the pattern is deferred (the factory does not offer it) and the colours are a standard range, so neither can carry recognition. The signature is therefore the consistent warm-neutral styling plus Lucy. Real-life version: you know an Anine Bing photo by its calm neutrals and by Anine herself before you read a word. We want the same instant recognition for Sportif: the look, and Lucy.

**Do now:** lock the visual system in `brand.md` and apply it identically to every band photo, pouch, Reel and email, with Lucy present in the content. Do not build recognition on a pattern or "custom colour" claim, neither is real.

---

## Lever 2: Lucy as the face (THE primary lever)

**The idea:** for a small brand the founder is the most powerful and least copyable asset. People follow people. **As of 2026-06-18 this is not just one lever of six, it is the differentiator** (Hugo confirmed). Because the product is a standard factory item, Lucy is the reason to buy.

**For Sportif:** Lucy is a Sydney PT and stylist with real press and a genuine origin story, she got sick of masculine black-and-fluro gym gear and made something feminine and beautiful. That story IS the brand. Integrating her personal account (@lucywayne__) and putting her on camera is the "Made by Lucy" angle from the brief, now the lead angle, and it directly serves her 10,000-follower goal. Real-life version: Kikiva grew on behind-the-scenes founder content; Anine Bing is the brand because she is the face of it. Sportif is the same model.

**Do now:** start a steady rhythm of Lucy-led content (the why, sampling, her picks, packing the gift pouch, unboxing the first 500 bands) in Kikiva warmth with Anine Bing restraint, and decide how @lucywayne__ connects to Sportif (the Lucy Wayne landing page funnelling to the brand).

---

## Lever 3: Stand for something

**The idea:** valuable brands mean something beyond the item, so people feel something when they buy.

**For Sportif:** the pretty, colourful, affordable-luxury answer to boring gym accessories, tied to the morning movement ritual, not just "it's pretty." Lucy's own line, "Too fashionable not to WEAR," is the playful flag. The sustainability promise (vegan, recycled, organic, the cotton pouch) is part of what we stand for, but only if it is a named, checkable program in the Aje Aware style, never vague "eco" words (that protects us from greenwashing and ACCC exposure, and our customer can spot a fake claim a mile off). Real-life version: people pay more for Patagonia because it stands for something, not because the jacket is cheaper to make.

**Do now:** lock one brand-promise line and repeat it everywhere. Hold the sustainability story until the material is chosen: as of 2026-06-18 Lucy is still deciding between recycled, organic, and hemp, so we cannot make a specific material claim yet. Once a material is locked, build the named program (Aje Aware style) around the real specifics.

---

## Lever 4: Build a community that feels ownership

**The idea:** the deepest moat is customers who feel part of it. They defend you, refer you, and will not switch to a cheaper dupe.

**For Sportif:** this matches Lucy's community-focused tone and her launch goals (10,000 followers, the Double Bay or Bondi launch event). **The mechanic Lucy chose (Session 011) is ambassador and instructor seeding:** get the bands onto Sydney pilates instructors and micro-creators who genuinely use them and post, and build the community the way the **440 Run Club** built its Bronte following (community-first, inclusive, eastern-suburbs beach culture, members visibly using the product). Lucy wants to emulate that model. Real-life version: the 440 grew from a handful of runners to 200-plus people every Saturday and 26k followers, with no fees and no ads, just a place people wanted to belong; that sense of belonging is the moat. Supporting mechanics to layer in later: reposting real customers, an ownable hashtag, and a colour or print vote (which doubles as engagement and feeds the product decision).

**Do now:** start the ambassador and instructor seeding plan: a short list of Sydney pilates instructors and micro-creators to gift bands to when the 500 land in July, and a simple "use it, tag us" ask. This is both the community play and the most cost-effective creator strategy for bands.

---

## Lever 5: Own the customer, do not rent them

**The idea:** Instagram followers and studio shelves are rented attention. The email list and your own store are owned. Owning the relationship is where brand value actually compounds, through repeat purchases.

**For Sportif:** this is the heart of the parallel wholesale-plus-DTC decision (settled Session 011). Wholesale through Lucy's gym relationships gets the band into studios and builds credibility, which is great, but a gym sale is a one-time handoff, we never meet that customer. So we run DTC at the same time, not later: the email list and the Shopify store are the assets that sell her the next colour, the strap, the cap, for years. The cotton pouch is our unboxing moment: it is a gift with purchase (free over a spend threshold of about $70), which both lifts average order value and makes a first order feel like a gift that earns a photo and a tag. Real-life version: a gym sells one band today; an owned email list sells the next three things to the same person over two years. That is where the value lives, which is exactly why DTC runs in parallel with wholesale rather than waiting behind it.

**Do now:** start a pre-launch email waitlist immediately (Klaviyo on Shopify), and seed it with Lucy's existing small contact list (she confirmed she has one). Give people a reason to sign up (early access or first dibs on a colourway). Make the pouch a real gift-with-purchase incentive (spend over the threshold, it is yours) and the unboxing genuinely beautiful. Run wholesale for reach, run email and DTC for equity, both from the start.

---

## Lever 6: Be consistent, keep quality high

**The idea:** brand value builds slowly with repetition and collapses fast with broken promises or constant discounting.

**For Sportif:** show up on a steady content cadence from now to September (the slow ramp Lucy wants, not one launch-day drop), make the band feel as good as it looks (Lucy buying a competitor band to check quality was exactly the right instinct), and hold prices. The warning sign is right there in our research: PE Nation and AJE get "pricey for the quality" complaints, and heavy discounting is the White Fox register we are avoiding. Real-life version: a brand that quietly holds its price feels worth more than one that is 40% off every other week.

**Do now:** set a simple weekly content rhythm, quality-check the first 500-unit run before it ships, and resist the urge to discount.

---

## Where to start (next 90 days, matched to the launch arc)

1. **Lock the look** (Lever 1). The brand kit is nearly there. Finish it, built on the warm-neutral styling plus Lucy, not on a pattern (deferred) or a "custom colour" claim (the colours are a standard factory range).
2. **Turn on Lucy-led content and the email waitlist now** (Levers 2 and 5). This is the "build" phase in the brief, now to July. Seed the waitlist with Lucy's existing contact list.
3. **Choose the one brand-promise line now; hold the sustainability story** (Lever 3) until the material is chosen, then write the named program around the real specifics.
4. **Start the ambassador and instructor seeding** (Lever 4), the mechanic Lucy chose, ready for when the 500 bands land in July. A colour or print vote can layer in later.
5. **Make the first order a beautiful experience** (Levers 5 and 6), the gift pouch and the unboxing.

---

## How we will know it is working

Not vanity numbers. Watch for: people recognising the look without the logo, real customers posting and tagging without being asked, the email list growing and opening (the leading indicator of launch-day sales), repeat purchases (the same person coming back for the strap or the next colour), and steady follower growth toward 10,000. Branded searches ("Sportif bands") are the sign the brand itself, not an ad, is pulling demand.

---

## Three calls this plan depends on (status after the 2026-06-18 meeting)

All three are now resolved (2026-06-18):

1. **The pattern (DEFERRED, closed).** Not happening for launch: the China manufacturer offers predefined colourways and no custom pattern. Colours are also a standard range, not bespoke. So neither is our signature. We win instead on Lucy, the experience, and the presentation, exactly the safe ground the devil's advocate review named. Revisit a pattern only if a future run makes it genuinely feasible.
2. **Hero product (SETTLED: the band).** The band is Lucy's first product and what is arriving, so it leads. Because the bare band is the most copied, cheapest-to-compare item, we sell it inside a styled set (band, strap, gift-with-purchase pouch), in a considered colour selection, carried by Lucy's story, so we never fight band-versus-band on price alone.
3. **Wholesale-led or consumer-led (SETTLED: parallel, both).** Not one before the other. Lucy works her existing gym relationships for warm wholesale placements while we build the DTC and email engine at the same time. Wholesale brings reach and credibility; DTC and email build the owned equity. The ambassador and instructor seeding feeds both.
