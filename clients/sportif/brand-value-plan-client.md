> Brand value is why someone chooses Sportif over a cheaper, almost identical band. The product can be copied. The brand cannot, and the brand is above all Lucy.

## 1. Lucy is the brand
*People follow people. For a small brand, the founder is the most powerful and least copyable asset.*

- Lucy is a Sydney PT and stylist with real press and a genuine story: tired of masculine black-and-fluro gym gear, she made something feminine and beautiful.
- Because the product itself is standard, Lucy is the reason to buy. Her taste and her story are the brand.
- The model is Anine Bing and Kikiva: the founder is the face, and people buy the world she builds.

**What we'll implement**
- A steady rhythm of Lucy-led content: the why, her picks, packing the pouch, unboxing the first bands.
- Connect @lucywayne__ to Sportif through a landing page that funnels to the brand.

## 2. One consistent, recognisable look
*A look so consistent that people know it is Sportif before they see the logo.*

- Warm neutrals (linen, cream, sand) with the product as the one pop of colour, in our palette.
- Lucy in the frame as a recurring face. The signature is the styling plus Lucy, not a pattern or a "custom colour" claim.

**What we'll implement**
- Lock the visual system and apply it identically to every photo, pouch, Reel and email.

## 3. Stand for something
*Valuable brands mean something beyond the item, so people feel something when they buy.*

- The pretty, affordable-luxury answer to boring gym gear, tied to the morning movement ritual. Lucy's line, "Too fashionable not to WEAR," is the flag.
- Sustainability is part of it, but only as a named, checkable program (Aje Aware style), never vague "eco" words the customer sees straight through.

**What we'll implement**
- Lock one brand-promise line and repeat it everywhere.
- Hold the sustainability story until the material is chosen, then build the named program around real specifics.

## 4. Build a community, not just a following
*The deepest moat is customers who feel part of it. They defend you, refer you, and will not switch to a cheaper dupe.*

- Seed the bands with Sydney pilates instructors and micro-creators who genuinely use them and post.
- Build it the way the 440 Run Club built its Bronte following: community-first, inclusive, members visibly using the product.

**What we'll implement**
- A short gifting list of instructors and micro-creators for when the first bands land, with a simple "use it, tag us" ask.

## 5. Own the customer, don't rent attention
*Followers and studio shelves are rented attention. The email list and the store are owned, and that is where value compounds.*

- Wholesale through Lucy's gyms brings reach and credibility, but a gym sale is a one-time handoff.
- So DTC runs at the same time: the email list and Shopify store sell her the next colour, the strap, the cap, for years.
- The gift-with-purchase pouch (free over about $70) lifts order value and makes a first order feel like a gift worth photographing.

**What we'll implement**
- Start a pre-launch email waitlist now (Klaviyo on Shopify), seeded with Lucy's contacts, with a reason to sign up.
- Make the pouch a real gift-with-purchase and the unboxing genuinely beautiful.

## 6. Show up consistently, keep quality high
*Brand value builds slowly with repetition and collapses fast with broken promises or constant discounting.*

- Show up on a steady cadence from now to September, not one launch-day drop.
- Make the band feel as good as it looks, and hold prices. Heavy discounting is the tone we avoid.

**What we'll implement**
- Set a simple weekly content rhythm, quality-check the first run before it ships, and resist discounting.

## What winning looks like
- People recognise the look without the logo.
- Customers post and tag Sportif without being asked.
- The email list grows and opens, the early sign of launch sales.
- Repeat purchases: the same person comes back for the strap or the next colour.
- Branded searches for "Sportif bands" start to appear.
