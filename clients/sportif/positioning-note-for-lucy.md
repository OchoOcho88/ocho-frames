# What we learned looking at the real products (and what it means for Sportif)

_A short note for Lucy. June 2026._

We went past the competitor websites and looked closely at what these brands actually sell: the real products, prices and colours. Here is the honest picture and what it means for how we position Sportif.

## The big correction

When we started, it looked like almost nobody sold a colourful band or ankle strap, so Sportif would have the space to itself. Looking closely, that is not quite true:

- **Booty bands** (Sportif's hero product) are sold by **Move Active** ($19.60 to $28, in soft colours) and **Your Reformer** ($29, in blue, sage, mocha and cream).
- **Ankle straps** are sold by **Avara** ($39, plain browns) and **Kikiva** ($29.99, in Pink and Baby Blue).

So both of Sportif's launch products already exist at well-known brands, and several already come in colour, at $29 to $39. That is more competition than it first looked, and it is much better to know now than after launch.

## Why this is actually good to know

It means "we are the colourful band brand" is not enough on its own, because two or three brands already sell colourful bands and straps. That is fine, because the more interesting truth still holds: for every one of these brands, the band or strap is a tiny side item buried in a big activewear range. None of them is built around it. None offers genuinely custom, distinctive colours (they use the same few pastels and neutrals). And none makes it feel like an affordable-luxury object you would want to leave on display.

## What makes Sportif different (the sharper version)

1. **Accessory-first.** The band, strap and pouch are the whole brand, the hero, not item 47 on a clothing site.
2. **Our own custom colours.** Not the same few pastels everyone has, but a distinctive Sportif palette no one else stocks.
3. **Affordable luxury, and vegan.** The cotton pouch, the elevated look, the vegan story, all presented like something special.
4. **Founder and community, done well** (your strength, and the thing Kikiva does that the others do not).

## The good surprises (real opportunities)

- **The pouch could be a product, not just packaging.** Anine Bing's branded pouch ($249) is sold out, and a brand called ODE sells a woven pouch at $70. People buy pretty pouches. Sportif's cotton pouch could be a hero item, even sold on its own.
- **Towels are in demand.** Move Active's mat and gym towels keep selling out. A colourful, design-led towel is an easy add after launch.
- **Caps and bags are the easy next step.** Almost every brand (Anine Bing, AJE, Move Active) makes good money from a logo cap collection and a small-to-large bag range. Natural extensions for Sportif once it is live.
- **Big players are validating the category.** The Nike x SKIMS collaboration now sells a pilates grip sock at $50. When the giants move in, it confirms the space is hot, and that there is room at a premium price.

## What this means we should do

- **Lead with focus and feel, not just colour.** The story is "the brand that makes the band, strap and pouch beautiful, in colours you cannot get anywhere else," not "we are the colourful one."
- **Nail the custom colours** so they are clearly ours and clearly different from Kikiva's pastels.
- **Treat the pouch as a hero**, not an afterthought.
- **Keep September tight** (bands, strap and pouch), and line up the easy wins (a colourful towel, a cap, a small bag) for after launch.

## Bottom line

Sportif is not walking into an empty room. But it can be the only brand in the room that is actually about these accessories, made beautifully, in its own colours, and treated as the main event. That is a stronger and more defensible position than "we are the colourful one," and it is very much winnable.
