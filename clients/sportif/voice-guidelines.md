# Sportif voice guidelines (checklist for every piece of content)

> Distilled from brand.md (Voice and Tone + Hard guardrails). Created 2026-07-07.
> Run every caption, email, ad, and page against this list before it ships.
> If brand.md's voice section changes, update this file.

## The voice in one line

Anine Bing's calm polish plus Kikiva's warmth: refined but friendly, confident but not cold, feminine and lively without being over the top.

## Two audiences, two registers

- **Customer-facing (Sportif copy):** short, calm, confident single lines. "Everyday training, elevated." Real founder warmth. Lucy's playful "Too fashionable not to WEAR!" energy used sparingly.
- **Lucy-facing (client docs):** plain English, "a mum can read it." No jargon, no acronyms without explanation, one-line ideas, tight bullets. Internal working notes never appear in her documents.

## Say (the vocabulary that works)

elevated, considered, for the long run, everyday training elevated, made to be seen, by Lucy, for your morning ritual, slow and beautiful. Clear calm CTAs on drops.

## Never say / never do

1. **No health-outcome or performance claims.** No "burns calories", "tones", "boosts", "booty gains". Aesthetic and lifestyle led. (This also keeps ads out of Meta's Restricted Health and Wellness bucket, which is triggered by claim language, not category.)
2. **No White Fox register.** No "obsessed", "babes", "must-have", urgency spam, exclamation-heavy hype, constant discount messaging, or skin-heavy imagery. Explicit anti-reference from Lucy.
3. **No sustainability or material claims yet.** Fabric is not locked (recycled / organic / hemp undecided). Any claim before that is greenwashing risk (ACCC). When locked, build a named program with checkable specifics (the AJE Aware model).
4. **No "custom colour" claims.** The colours are a curated selection from the manufacturer's standard range, not bespoke.
5. **No gym-bro tone, no slangy TikTok-teen voice.** Stay one notch more refined than the typical small activewear label.
6. **Body-focused product names are fine ("booty band"), body-targeting ad copy is not.**
7. **No em dashes, no en dashes.** Anywhere. Use commas, periods, or parentheses. Sweep AI-generated text before saving, it reintroduces them.
8. **Nothing logo- or label-dependent as a final** until trademark clearance is confirmed.

## Imagery rules

Warm-neutral palette (blush peach #F0CDB3, caramel, terracotta, chocolate, linen). Tasteful, elevated, never skin-heavy. Fonts: Glacial Indifference is the real brand font; in the sandbox use letter-spaced Poppins for the wordmark and Lora for editorial titles.
