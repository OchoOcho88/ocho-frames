## 7. The spend level where incremental revenue starts to flatten

There is no single universal threshold; it depends on:

- Audience size (Australia only vs global)  
- Creative breadth and fatigue  
- Operational constraints (inventory, fulfilment)  

For a **small, founder‑led AU fitness accessory brand** focused on IG + TikTok in 2026, a useful way to think about it is:

### Conceptual rule of thumb (estimate)

When:

- Your *blended* MER (total revenue / total paid media spend) starts dropping past **3.0 to 3.5** and continues to fall with each additional budget increase, and  
- You are already:  
  - Testing at least 5 to 10 strong creative variants per month  
  - Utilizing retargeting and email flows  
  - Maintaining product availability  

you are close to your **current efficient frontier**.

### Practical spend thresholds for this brand size (estimate)

Under the conditions described in your brief:

- **Learning / early stage (up to ~10k/month):**  
  - You are unlikely to hit saturation across AU.  
  - Incremental spend will often still improve total revenue, as you are simply under‑spending relative to the reachable audience.

- **Mid stage (~15k to 30k/month):**  
  - This is where most small AU brands start encountering **rising CAC** if they do not add enough fresh creative and new audiences.  
  - You may see diminishing incremental revenue per extra 5k once you cross **20k to 25k AUD/month** if your creative pipeline is light.

- **Scale stage (50k+ per month):**  
  - For a niche product like resistance booty bands and ankle straps, purely AU‑only, you are quite likely to approach **diminishing returns** somewhere in the **50k to 80k AUD/month** range unless:  
    - You aggressively expand to broader lifestyle / wellness audiences, and  
    - You have a large variety of creative angles and offers.  
  - Past this point, each additional 10k may produce proportionally less revenue increase, and CAC will climb.

### Practical benchmark to manage this (estimate)

- Track **weekly MER** and **new customer CAC**.  
- If you raise monthly spend by **20 to 30 percent** and see:  
  - MER drop more than **15 to 20 percent** and  
  - CAC rise more than **20 to 30 percent**  
  for 3 to 4 weeks in a row despite active creative testing, you are likely above the point where additional spend is efficient for your current stage.

For your brand, planning a 2026 AU launch then US expansion in 2027, a **sensible ceiling** in AU before shifting attention to the US test would be:

- **Around 30k/month while you are pre‑US**, with scale testing up to **50k+** only once you see stable ROAS and strong product‑market fit.
