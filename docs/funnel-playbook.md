# Funnel Playbook

> How to build funnels that sell products. Foundations for Sportif and every future business.
> Source: Hugo's notes from the Australian Marketing Summit 2026 (presented by Ethan Donati and Jane Lu, 2026-07-10), organised and cross-checked against 2026 benchmarks and research. Donati's book is "The Neuroscience Behind 7 Figure Funnels" (neuromarketing applied to funnel building).
> Sportif-specific application lives in `clients/sportif/funnel-plan.md`. This doc is the reusable layer.
> **Created:** 2026-07-11 (Session 017, Cowork)

## 1. What a funnel actually is

A funnel is "do you want fries with that", online. One offer at a time, one choice at a time, one page per idea (Donati's reference: the Qantas booking flow, which asks you exactly one question per screen).

The core rules:

- **Send ads to funnels, never to websites.** A website is a menu with fifty choices; a funnel is one question. Every ad points at a page built for exactly one action.
- **One funnel per offer.** Do not make a single funnel sell two things.
- **Hyper-targeted positioning.** Written for one person, distributed to many. One target per target market.
- **Foundation first: solve a problem.** Know the target market's pain point before anything else. If the offer does not solve a problem (functional or emotional), no funnel structure saves it.

## 2. The four elements of a high-converting funnel page

Donati's checklist, confirmed by 2026 landing page research:

1. **Opt-in box** (capture name, number, email; the goal of the page is the database entry or the sale, nothing else).
2. **Result-based headline** (see the formula below).
3. **Short video, 2 to 5 minutes** (founder to camera beats produced ads; more below).
4. **Social proof** (testimonials framed as outcome + objection: "where were you, where are you now").

Plus: **mobile-first view**. Design the page for a phone, then check desktop, not the reverse.

## 3. The headline formula

**How to [outcome they want] without [their biggest objection].**

Donati's example: "How to get a 6 pack without giving up your favourite foods." Be specific on both halves. The outcome half comes from the pain point research; the objection half comes from the false belief work in section 6.

Caution for regulated or claim-sensitive categories (health, fitness, finance): the outcome half must not be a prohibited claim. Reframe to an aesthetic, lifestyle, or convenience outcome. (This matters directly for Sportif, see the client plan.)

## 4. What a funnel requires: the offer

A funnel converts an offer; it does not create one. The offer needs:

1. **A compelling incentive** (the reason to act: a gift with purchase, a bundle, a free resource, a quote or consult).
2. **Urgency and scarcity** (honest only: a real drop size, a real restock lead time, a real event date. Fake countdown timers erode trust and violate consumer law in Australia).
3. **A special offer** (the packaging of 1 and 2 into one clear promise).
4. **Testimonials** structured as outcomes and answered objections.
5. Reference models Donati cited: Koala bedding and HelloFresh, both of which run permanent structured acquisition offers rather than random discounts.

Offer creation is also where you address the **false belief or misconception** blocking the sale. Three layers (Donati's vehicle / internal / external framing):

- **Vehicle:** "this method (or this brand or founder) won't work" → prove the method with results and demonstration.
- **Internal:** "it won't work for ME" (money, time, skill) → show people like them succeeding.
- **External:** "something outside me will stop it" (partner, budget, time) → remove friction, reduce risk (guarantee, low entry price).

## 5. The full journey: ad to sale to scale

The sequence from the notes, cleaned into an 11-step spine:

1. **Foundation:** target market, pain points, one problem to solve.
2. **Hook message:** stop the scroll. The first 1 to 2 seconds earn the next ten.
3. **Story:** people buy emotionally, and "no one cares about you yet." The story arc that works: where I was (lowlights), how I overcame it, my results. One minute of "where are you now" relatability before any pitch.
4. **Educational marketing** for long-term trust: answer real FAQs, one ad per question. Every ad answers "what's in it for me."
5. **Launch ads → funnel:** capture name, number, email.
6. **Email follow-up** (automated flows, not manual sends).
7. **SMS follow-up** (the fastest-growing follow-up channel; pair with email rather than replacing it).
8. **Disqualifying funnel** where relevant (a short quiz, up to 10 questions, that qualifies the lead and personalises the pitch; more useful for services and high-ticket than for a $30 product).
9. **Retargeting ads:** always give a NEXT action to warm traffic, never re-run the cold ad.
10. **Sales.**
11. **Scale fast** once cost per acquisition is known and profitable.

**The governing metric: how much can you spend to acquire a customer?** Everything above exists to make CAC knowable, then beatable. Track cost per lead, cost per sale, and the brand-building spend separately.

## 6. Neuromarketing techniques that make funnels convert

- **People buy emotionally, then justify rationally.** Pepsi vs Coke: the product is near-identical, the feeling is the difference. Sell trust, safety, identity.
- **Affinity-based buying:** what does it say about the buyer when they buy from you? (Nike sells "achieving greatness," not shoes.) Design the funnel so the purchase is a self-statement.
- **Help customers decide.** Decision fatigue kills conversion. Fewer options, clearer defaults, one call to action per page.
- **Green-light questions, not dead-end statements.** "Would Tuesday or Thursday suit better?" converts; "let me know if you're interested" dies. In copy: end sections with a question the reader answers yes to.
- **Price point framing:** anchor against the alternative (the cost of not solving the problem, or the pricier competitor), not against your own cost.
- **Packaging matters.** The unboxing is part of the product. Premium feel comes from presentation as much as the item.

## 7. Channels: what each is for

| Channel | Role | Notes from the summit |
|---|---|---|
| Meta (Facebook + Instagram) | The core paid engine. "Dominate Meta." | Media buying is a learnable button-pressing skill. Best quick results. |
| Google Ads | Search intent, product searches | Use when people already search for the category. |
| TikTok | Cheapest reach, fun content environment | Native, unpolished creative wins. |
| YouTube ads | Quick results tier alongside Meta and Google | Longer-form story ads. |
| ChatGPT / AI-surface ads | New in 2026, early | Watch, small tests only. |
| Podcasts, blogging, YouTube organic | Long-term marketing | Compounding trust, not quick wins. |

**Creative rules:** no stock photos, ever. The best ad is real, interactive, high-touch, human. Founder-shot phone video outperforms agency polish for small brands (2026 research: founder presence lifts landing page conversion roughly 15 to 28 percent versus anonymous brand pages).

## 8. 2026 benchmarks (so we know what "working" looks like)

Rounded from current published data; treat as orientation, not gospel.

- **Landing page conversion by source:** paid social 1 to 3 percent (dedicated cold-traffic pages 2.5 to 4), paid search 2 to 5, email traffic 4 to 8, organic 1.5 to 4.
- **The biggest leak is before add-to-cart:** roughly 93 of 100 visitors leave without adding anything, and about half of started checkouts abandon. Stores with add-to-cart above 10 percent tend to convert above 3.8 percent overall.
- **Email flows:** welcome flows open around 80 percent and average roughly $2.65 revenue per recipient; abandoned-cart flows open around 50 percent, recover 8 to 12 percent at the top end, and average roughly $3.65 per recipient. Flows generate about 41 percent of email revenue from about 5 percent of sends. Automate first, campaign second.
- **Email then SMS:** cart email at the one-hour mark, SMS about 24 hours later if unopened.
- **Meta lead ads vs landing page:** instant forms come in 30 to 50 percent cheaper per lead and fill 2 to 3x more, but landing-page leads are higher intent. Use instant forms for volume list-building, landing pages for buyers.
- **Lead cost (AU):** most industries land between roughly $3 and $30 per lead on Meta; respond to leads within minutes, not hours (5-minute response time massively lifts qualification).
- **Minimum viable ad budget:** roughly $30 to $50 per day per campaign so the algorithm gets data; run tests 7 to 10 days before judging.

## 9. The mindset layer (worth keeping)

From the summit's softer content, kept because it is operationally real for founder brands:

- "Be your best self on the worst day." Founder-led brands live and die on consistency of showing up.
- Mindset content (inspirational, quote-led video) is a legitimate top-of-funnel format for a female-audience founder brand, not fluff. It is affinity-based buying in action.
- Positioning: be the specialist, not the generalist. The market pays the specialist.

## 10. Checklist for any new funnel

1. Who exactly is it for, and what one problem does it solve?
2. What is the one action this page asks for?
3. Headline: how to [outcome] without [objection]?
4. Is there a real incentive, real urgency, and a special offer?
5. Founder video 2 to 5 minutes?
6. Social proof as outcome + objection?
7. Mobile-first?
8. Email flow AND SMS follow-up wired before ads go live?
9. Retargeting with a NEXT action ready?
10. Do we know the target CAC and the kill criteria before spending?
